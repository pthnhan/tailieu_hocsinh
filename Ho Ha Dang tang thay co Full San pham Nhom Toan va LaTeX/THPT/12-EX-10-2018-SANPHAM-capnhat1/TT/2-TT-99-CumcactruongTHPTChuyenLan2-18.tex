 Cho hàm số $y=f(x)$ có đạo hàm liên tục trên $[0;1]$ và thỏa mãn $f(1)=0$; $\displaystyle\int\limits_{0}^1 [f'(x)]^2 \mathrm{\,d}x=\displaystyle\int\limits_0^1 (x+1)\mathrm{e}^xf(x)\mathrm{\,d}x=\dfrac{\mathrm{e}^2-1}{4}$. Tính $\displaystyle\int\limits_0^1 f(x)\mathrm{\,d}x$.
\choice
{$\dfrac{\mathrm{e}}{2}$}
{$\dfrac{\mathrm{e}-1}{2}$}
{$\dfrac{\mathrm{e}^2}{4}$}
{\True $2-\mathrm{e}$}
\loigiai{
Đặt $\heva{& u=f(x) \\& \mathrm{\,d}v=(x+1)\mathrm{e}^x \mathrm{d}x}\Rightarrow \heva{& \mathrm{\,d}u=f'(x)\mathrm{\,d}x \\& v=x\mathrm{e}^x}$.\\

Xét
$\displaystyle\int\limits_0^1 [f'(x)+x\mathrm{e}^x]^2\mathrm{\,d}x=\displaystyle\int\limits_0^1 \left[f'(x)\right]^2\mathrm{\,d}x+2\displaystyle\int\limits_0^1 x\cdot \mathrm{e}^xf'(x)\mathrm{\,d}x+\displaystyle\int\limits_0^1 x^2\mathrm{e}^{2x}\mathrm{\,d}x=0$. \\
$\Rightarrow f'(x)=-x\mathrm{e}^x\Rightarrow f(x)=-\displaystyle\int\limits {x\mathrm{e}^x\mathrm{\, d}x}=(1-x)\mathrm{e}^x+C.$\\
Mà $f(1)=0$ nên $C=0$.\\
Do đó $\displaystyle\int\limits_0^1 f(x)\mathrm{\,d}x=\displaystyle\int\limits_0^1 \left(1-x\right)\mathrm{e}^x\mathrm{\,d}x=(2-x)\mathrm{e}^x\bigg|_0^1=2-\mathrm{e} $.
}
